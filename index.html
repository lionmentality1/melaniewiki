This Wikipedia-style page was created as part of a group assignment focused on researching a present-day leader. Our group chose Melanie Kirwa as an example of an effective leader in the field of education.

Melanie Kirwa is an educator and author working in higher education, known for her supportive teaching style and leadership in international learning environments. She has experience working with students from different cultural backgrounds, which has strengthened her communication and mentoring skills.

She is affiliated with HAMK University of Applied Sciences, where she works as a teacher tutor and thesis language specialist, supporting students in their academic development and personal growth.

Her leadership approach is based on empathy, trust, and open communication, making her a respected and inspiring leader among students and colleagues.

Basic background information

Melanie Kirwa (born November 1972) is a Canadian educator and author specializing in Organizational Communication and international education. She was born in the small town of Renfrew, Canada.

She is affiliated with HAMK University of Applied Sciences, where she serves in mentorship and supervisory roles, including working as a teacher tutor and a thesis language specialist.

In addition to her work in higher education, Kirwa has entrepreneurial experience as a former owner of a language training firm serving the Finnish business sector. She has also co-authored five published children’s books, reflecting her background in cultural anthropology and commitment to diversity.

Leadership qualifications

Kirwa’s professional leadership is supported by advanced academic credentials. She completed an undergraduate degree in English Literature and Cultural Anthropology, followed by a Master’s degree and a Licentiate Diploma in TESL.

Her leadership qualifications also include practical experience in mentoring students and supervising academic work, as well as entrepreneurial leadership in language training.

Leadership style

The supportive and people-oriented leadership style of Melanie Kirwa is communicated, trusted, and created through a calm environment within the classroom. Rather than using an authoritarian style of control over her students, she provides them with clear guidance and allows them to feel secure in their abilities.

In addition to communicating, trusting, and creating a calm environment with her students, she is also flexible. She adjusts her approach to accommodate various levels of student learning (adult and child), and she uses different methods of delivery based on the type of students she is working with. She creates a positive learning environment for all students, paying attention to their cultural differences and ensuring that they all feel comfortable and included.

Lastly, she demonstrates her leadership style by "leading through example." She listens carefully before responding, communicates clearly, and uses respectful communication toward others regardless of different opinions. As a result, her students are more open, participate more in their classes, and have less fear of making mistakes.

Achievements

Melanie Kirwa's results reflect her long-standing commitment to education and her ability to mentor students. She helps students achieve academic goals by providing academic guidance and assistance with writing and thesis language, demonstrating real leadership through supporting academic success.

Moreover, she has extensive experience beyond the classroom. Melanie's entrepreneurial background, including running a language training company, demonstrates her ability to take responsibility, manage time effectively, and achieve results in a professional environment.

Furthermore, Melanie has co-authored five published children’s books together with other authors. This achievement shows her creativity, discipline, and ability to communicate ideas clearly, which are also important leadership skills.

Distinctive qualities

Melanie is a motivational educator who builds trust and respect among her students. When a student expresses what she may refer to as an “incorrect perspective,” she does not shut them down. Instead, she encourages discussion and allows students to share their own opinions and thoughts without fear of being judged.

Melanie inspires others through her warm and genuine personality. Her positive, friendly, and approachable attitude makes students feel comfortable asking questions. When students feel comfortable, they learn faster and develop greater confidence.

Another distinctive quality of Melanie’s leadership is her encouragement of courage and independent thinking. She supports students who want to improve through experimentation, innovation, and developing their own perspectives.

Helping students become confident by never accusing others of wrong opinions or thinking. She encourages open discussion and supports students in expressing their ideas freely.

Being positive and friendly makes students feel safe to ask questions and participate actively in class.

Encouraging students to be brave and different in their thinking, which helps them grow both academically and personally.

Ethics and principles

She prioritizes the interests of her colleagues, students, and the campus community and ensures that everyone works together as a strong team at HAMK.

She ensures that her actions and words build confidence and mutual respect within the learning environment.

Melanie has gained strong trust among students, which makes them feel comfortable speaking with her whenever they need guidance or support.

Leadership qualities

Melanie is compassionate and a well-spoken leader who is also a strong team player.

She encourages students to collaborate, respect each other, and work together toward common goals.

She builds accountability among students and is courageous enough to stand up and speak on behalf of her students when needed.

Her supportive nature helps students feel motivated and confident in their abilities.

Areas for improvement

Melanie Kirwa is a strong leader, but she could become even better by sharing more of her experience outside the classroom.

Writing more academic articles or joining international education projects could help her reach a wider audience and influence more educators and students.

Taking larger leadership roles at the university level could also allow her skills and knowledge to support more people in the academic community.
