Basic background information

Melanie Kirwa (born November 1972) is a Canadian educator and author specializing in Organizational Communication and international education. She was born in the small town of Renfrew, Canada.

She is affiliated with HAMK University of Applied Sciences, where she serves in mentorship and supervisory roles, including working as a teacher tutor and a thesis language specialist.

In addition to her work in higher education, Kirwa has entrepreneurial experience as a former owner of a language training firm serving the Finnish business sector. She has also co-authored five published children’s books, reflecting her background in cultural anthropology and commitment to diversity.

Leadership qualifications

Kirwa’s professional leadership is supported by advanced academic credentials. She completed an undergraduate degree in English Literature and Cultural Anthropology, followed by a Master’s degree and a Licentiate Diploma in TESL.

Her leadership qualifications also include practical experience in mentoring students and supervising academic work, as well as entrepreneurial leadership in language training.
